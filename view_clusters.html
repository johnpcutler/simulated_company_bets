Principal Component Analysis

This visualization shows the clustering of initiatives in 2D space using Principal Component Analysis (PCA). Each point represents an initiative, colored by its cluster assignment. The black X markers indicate cluster centroids. The explained variance ratio shows how much of the data's variance is captured by these two principal components.

Metric Distribution by Cluster

These box plots show the distribution of scores for each evaluation metric across the clusters. The boxes show the quartiles, with the middle line representing the median. Whiskers extend to show the rest of the distribution, and points represent outliers. This helps identify which metrics differentiate the clusters.

Metric Correlations

The correlation matrix shows the relationships between different evaluation metrics. Red indicates positive correlations, while blue indicates negative correlations. The strongest correlations are:

  • Solution Risk ↔ Solution Confidence (0.834) - These metrics are measuring the same concept: Solution Risk (1=unlikely to work, 10=will work) and Solution Confidence (1=low faith, 10=high faith) show strong agreement
  • Overall Size ↔ Lag For Impact (0.791) - Shorter initiatives tend to have shorter lag times before impact
  • Iteration Friendliness ↔ Experimentation Friendliness (0.785) - These metrics are strongly related, suggesting they often go hand in hand
  • Overall Size ↔ Experimentation Friendliness (0.772) - Shorter initiatives tend to be more experimentation-friendly
  • Iteration Friendliness ↔ Overall Size (0.715) - Shorter initiatives tend to be more iteration-friendly

Note: The Overall Size metric is scored from 1 (long initiative) to 10 (short initiative). The positive correlations with experimentation and iteration friendliness suggest that shorter, more focused initiatives are easier to experiment with and iterate on.
